Project 03

Office Group Order Optimization

Modeling the As-Is vs To-Be process of office group ordering to eliminate payment bottlenecks.

Process AnalystRole
1 WeekTimeline
Draw.io, BPMN 2.0Tools

1. The Problem

At the office, the daily process of ordering group lunches or milk tea is a logistical nightmare. The process flows through a group chat where people constantly change their minds, forget to pay, and force the Admin to manually reconcile orders and shipping fees.

This manual coordination results in a high "Payment Deficit" (Admin losing money) and a severe waste of productive work hours.

2. Constraints & Trade-offs

The Constraint: Third-party food delivery apps (like ShopeeFood or Grab) have group order features, but they often lack flexible payment splitting and don't allow the Admin to collect funds before placing the order.

The Trade-off: Instead of relying on rigid third-party apps, I modeled a custom To-Be process using a simple internal form linked to a QR payment gateway. The trade-off was a slight learning curve for staff, but absolute financial security for the Admin.

3. Approach & BA Artifacts

A. Process Modeling (As-Is)
I mapped the current state using BPMN 2.0. The diagram clearly highlighted the "Payment Collection" node as the critical bottleneck. Admin was advancing cash and acting as a debt collector post-delivery.

B. Bottleneck Detection
By timing the process, I found the Admin spent an average of 45 minutes per order just tracing chat messages to match drinks with faces, and calculating the divided shipping fee.

C. Process Optimization (To-Be)
I engineered a new flow: System calculates exact split -> Staff pays via QR -> System verifies 100% funds -> Order placed. No cash advanced, zero risk.

4. Core System Functions & UI Design

Beyond the interface, I defined strict functional requirements for the MVP:

  • Group Ordering Host: An initiator creates a session, and the system generates a sharable joining link for team members.
  • Auto-Consolidation & Split: The system aggregates item choices, calculates subtotal, applies discounts, and fairly divides the shipping fee.
  • Dynamic QR Payment: Automatically generates a bank QR code embedding the exact split amount and a predefined transfer syntax (e.g., [User]_Pay_OrderID).

I utilized process visualization to pitch the new workflow to the team, demonstrating the exact elimination of the bottleneck via a Neo-Brutalism interface design.

Real-world UI/UX Benchmark

An internal office tool demands raw efficiency over form. I chose Neo-Brutalism and Retro aesthetics to emphasize stark functionality and hyper-contrast:

  • Gumroad: The "textbook" for Neo-Brutalism. Inspired the aggressive 2px solid black borders, hard offset shadows, and high-contrast color palette (like Lavender Rose) to make actions unmistakable.
  • PostHog: Inspired the retro, highly functional aesthetic that makes technical/administrative tasks feel less dry and more engaging.
  • Yale School of Art: Inspired the raw, asymmetrical layouts and straightforward typography that prioritize truth and utility.

5. BA Deliverables Deep Dive

A. BPMN 2.0 Process Modeling

Using professional tools like Camunda / Visual Paradigm, I rigorously mapped the "As-Is" and "To-Be" states. The analysis revealed that the manual process contained 3 unnecessary intermediate steps (Tracing chat → Calculating fee → Debt collection). The "To-Be" automated process cut these out, directly reducing the coordination time from 45 minutes to just 5 minutes.

B. Sequence Diagram (UML) - Payment Integration

To demonstrate a deep understanding of 3-tier architecture and system integration, I modeled the payment flow logic:

User (UI)
├── 1. Click "Confirm & Pay"
└── 2. Request sent to Backend
Backend (Logic)
├── 3. Validate total split amount
├── 4. Call E-Wallet API (POST /v1/payment/qr)
└── 5. Wait for API Response

├── 6. Generate secure Transaction ID
└── 7. Return Dynamic QR Code Data
Backend (Logic)
└── 8. Parse QR Data & save to Database
User (UI)
└── 9. Render Dynamic QR Code on screen

C. Software Requirements Specification (SRS) Excerpt

Following IEEE 830 / ISO 29148 standards, I drafted a structured SRS highlighting strict Business Rules for financial accuracy.

3.1.2 Business Rules (BR)

  • BR-01 (Order Cutoff): The system SHALL automatically lock the ordering session at the Host-defined cutoff time. Late entries SHALL be rejected to prevent shipping fee recalculation.
  • BR-02 (Fee Distribution): The total shipping fee and external discounts SHALL be divided equally among all unique participating users, regardless of individual order volume.
  • BR-03 (Payment Verification): The Host SHALL NOT be allowed to trigger the "Place Order to Vendor" action until the system registers 100% of required funds transferred via the Dynamic QR gateway.

6. Business Impact & Results

Modeling and optimizing this micro-process yielded immediate, measurable ROI for the team.

  • Reduced Admin coordination time from 45 minutes to 5 minutes (80% efficiency gain).
  • Eliminated payment deficits entirely (0% loss rate).

Key Takeaway: Business Process Modeling Notation (BPMN) isn't just for factory floors. Applying it to everyday office logistics proves that a strong BA can detect inefficiencies and architect logical solutions anywhere.
