FlowCRM: Streamlining B2B Sales Pipelines

A comprehensive enterprise CRM solution designed to eliminate manual data entry, optimize lead tracking, and empower sales managers with real-time analytics.

Pro Tip: Data, branding, and specific UI elements have been anonymized or recreated as dummy assets to comply with Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDA).
Domain B2B CRM / SaaS
Role Business Analyst & UI/UX Designer
Target Users Sales Managers, Sales Reps

1. The Business Problem (As-Is)

Prior to FlowCRM, the client relied heavily on disconnected Excel spreadsheets and scattered email threads to manage their B2B sales funnel. This resulted in significant operational pain-points:

  • Lead Leakage: Approximately 20% of qualified leads were dropped due to lack of automated follow-up reminders.
  • Inefficient Reporting: Sales Managers spent an average of 5-7 hours per week manually consolidating pipeline data.
  • Lack of Visibility: No centralized system existed to track the historical interactions with a client, leading to overlapping communications.

2. Scope & Constraints

To ensure a rapid time-to-market and validate core assumptions, we strictly defined the Minimum Viable Product (MVP) scope:

  • Core Focus: Lead capturing, Kanban-style pipeline management, and basic activity logging.
  • Constraints: The system needed to integrate seamlessly with their legacy ERP system within a strict 3-month development timeline. Advanced AI forecasting was pushed to Phase 2.

Elicitation Approach: Conducted semi-structured interviews with 3 Regional Sales Managers and distributed a digital survey to 25 Sales Representatives to precisely map out their daily bottlenecks.

3. Business Analysis Artifacts

Bridging the gap between the sales team's chaotic workflow and the development team's technical architecture required rigorous documentation.

BPMN 2.0: Lead Conversion Process (As-Is vs To-Be)
Modeled the To-Be process to highlight automated triggers (e.g., status changes triggering email notifications). This artifact was crucial for aligning stakeholders on the new optimized workflow before writing any code.
Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD) Data Modeling
Designed the logical data model mapping out the relationships between Users, Leads, Opportunities, Companies, and Activity Logs. This ensured developers had a clear schema blueprint.
User Stories & Acceptance Criteria Product Backlog
Wrote Epics and User Stories using the standard Gherkin syntax (Given/When/Then) to define unambiguous Acceptance Criteria. E.g., "As a Sales Rep, I want to drag and drop a Lead card to the 'Negotiation' column, so that the pipeline probability updates automatically."

4. Proposed Solution & UI Design

The final MVP delivered a clean, intuitive, and high-performance interface. I utilized Figma to build high-fidelity wireframes that visually translated the business logic.

  • Kanban Pipeline: A visual drag-and-drop board that dramatically reduced the cognitive load for tracking deals.
  • Activity Timeline: A centralized view of all emails, calls, and meetings tied to a specific client.

Post-launch metrics indicated a highly successful adoption by the sales team, directly impacting the bottom line.

Lessons Learned

  • Managing Scope Creep: Stakeholders frequently requested complex automation rules during MVP development. I successfully utilized the "MoSCoW" prioritization technique to defer non-critical features to the Product Backlog.
  • Dev-Business Alignment: Regular grooming sessions with the technical lead were essential to ensure the ERD and API capabilities matched the UI design requirements.
